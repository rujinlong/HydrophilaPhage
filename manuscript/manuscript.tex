\begin{abstract}
\noindent
\emph{Aeromonas hydrophila} is a major aquaculture pathogen with rising
multidrug resistance, motivating phage-based alternatives to antibiotics.
We isolated and characterized a novel bacteriophage, vB\_AhyM\_Hp3 (Hp3).
Hp3 has a lytic cycle with a latent period of approximately 45 minutes and
is stable across a wide range of temperatures (4--45\,\textdegree C) and
at pH 6.0--9.0, with a one-log reduction in titer at pH 10.0. Its 84.75 kb double-stranded DNA genome (GC content 59\%)
shows the hallmarks of a lytic, non-temperate lifestyle and lacks known
antimicrobial-resistance or known virulence genes. Taxonomic and
comparative analyses place Hp3 in a highly distinct, currently
unclassified \emph{Caudoviricetes} genus; annotated nucleotide-metabolism
and other candidate genes await experimental validation. In a largemouth
bass (\emph{Micropterus salmoides}) model, a single dose at a multiplicity
of infection of 1.0 raised seven-day survival under lethal challenge from
15\% to 85\%, while fish receiving phage alone showed 100\% survival
without overt pathology. Hp3 is a genetically
distinctive candidate for aquaculture biocontrol and a starting point for
mechanistic and formulation studies.
\end{abstract}
