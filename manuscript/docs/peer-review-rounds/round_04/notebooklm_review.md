## NotebookLM answer (verbatim)

Here is the rigorous, novelty-focused peer review of the manuscript:

**R4-01: IN-VIVO NOVELTY VS. PRIOR ART**
*   **(a) Claim/line attacked:** Lines 21–25 (Abstract), 256–273, and 312–317 (Conclusion) asserting the *in vivo* rescue of largemouth bass from *A. hydrophila* as a major breakthrough. 
*   **(b) Why it is vulnerable:** The authors write as if curing an *A. hydrophila* infection in fish with a phage is an unprecedented phenomenon. It is not. They entirely fail to cite a massive body of prior literature demonstrating the efficacy of *Aeromonas* phage therapy in fish. For example, recent studies have achieved up to 100% survival in rohu carp using phage AhFM11. By ignoring the existing *in vivo* prior art, the authors artificially inflate the novelty of their findings, presenting confirmatory results as groundbreaking discoveries.
*   **(c) Corpus citation:** Muliya Sankappa et al., 2024 ("Novel lytic bacteriophage AhFM11 as an effective therapy against hypervirulent *Aeromonas hydrophila*"); Ramos-Vivas et al., 2021 ("Phage Therapy as a Focused Management Strategy in Aquaculture").
*   **(d) Specific revision required:** **[must-fix]** The authors must cite prior *A. hydrophila* *in vivo* phage therapy studies (such as the AhFM11 trials). They must explicitly state in the Discussion that their *in vivo* results are confirmatory of the established phage therapy paradigm in aquaculture, and compare Hp3's performance against these previously published benchmarks.

**R4-02: THE NOVELTY HINGES ON TAXONOMY — WHICH IS CONTESTED**
*   **(a) Claim/line attacked:** Lines 17–18, 275–278, and 318–322 heavily promoting that Hp3 represents a "putative new viral family".
*   **(b) Why it is vulnerable:** As noted in earlier review rounds, elevating a single, two-genome genus to a "new family" without sufficient sampling or an official ICTV proposal is taxonomically unwarranted. Because the authors use this inflated taxonomic claim as the primary hook for the paper, stripping it away leaves the manuscript searching for its actual core contribution. The novelty must stand on the functional and genomic distinctness of the phage, not on premature taxonomic grandstanding.
*   **(c) Corpus citation:** Turner et al., 2021 ("A Roadmap for Genome-Based Phage Taxonomy"); Bujak et al., 2022 (demonstrating that discovering highly divergent, unclassified *Aeromonas* phages is a common occurrence).
*   **(d) Specific revision required:** **[must-fix]** Force the authors to plainly state the single defensible novel contribution of the paper in the Abstract and Conclusion: the genomic characterization of a new, deeply divergent *genus* of *Aeromonas* phage with validated *in vivo* efficacy. The reliance on the contested "new family" crutch to force novelty must be removed.

**R4-03: STANDARD CHARACTERIZATION ≠ NOVELTY**
*   **(a) Claim/line attacked:** Lines 174–212 (Sections 3.1 to 3.3), detailing TEM, optimal MOI, the one-step growth curve, and stability assays.
*   **(b) Why it is vulnerable:** These assays are routine, descriptive prerequisites required for literally every phage isolation paper published today. Presenting the outputs of standard microbiological assays as if they are major, novel scientific breakthroughs dilutes the manuscript's actual value and reads as filler.
*   **(c) Corpus citation:** Bujak et al., 2022; Muliya Sankappa et al., 2024.
*   **(d) Specific revision required:** **[strengthening]** Condense the presentation of the standard characterizations. Reframe these sections strictly as validating the phage's baseline suitability for downstream use, rather than treating routine assay outputs as novel biological discoveries.

**R4-04: MISSING COMPARATORS**
*   **(a) Claim/line attacked:** Lines 189–202, reporting a latent period of ~45 min, burst size, and thermal/pH stability without any comparative context. 
*   **(b) Why it is vulnerable:** The authors provide raw numbers but fail to benchmark Hp3 against published *Aeromonas* phages. For instance, a 45-minute latent period is arguably quite slow compared to the 10-minute latent period of phage AhFM11. Furthermore, Hp3's host range is extremely narrow (lysing only 2 of 19 strains). Without a comparative baseline in the text, the journal's readership cannot evaluate whether Hp3 is objectively superior, competitive, or inferior as a biocontrol agent.
*   **(c) Corpus citation:** Muliya Sankappa et al., 2024; Bujak et al., 2022.
*   **(d) Specific revision required:** **[must-fix]** Insert a comparative table or a dedicated paragraph in the Discussion benchmarking Hp3's biological parameters (latent period, burst size, host range breadth, and stability) directly against recently characterized *Aeromonas* phages (e.g., AhFM11, Bujak's phages). 

**R4-05: OVERCLAIM: "PLATFORM FOR NEXT-GENERATION THERAPIES"**
*   **(a) Claim/line attacked:** Lines 25–26, 320–322, and 328–330, claiming Hp3 is a "valuable platform for engineering next-generation phage therapies" and "engineering the next generation of intelligent, evolution-informed therapeutics."
*   **(b) Why it is vulnerable:** This is evidence-free, aspirational buzzword bingo. The authors performed zero genetic engineering, zero receptor-binding protein (RBP) modification, and zero synthetic biology. Simply sequencing a genome does not suddenly make the isolate an "engineering platform." 
*   **(c) Corpus citation:** Dunne et al., 2021 ("Reprogramming bacteriophage host range - Design principles and strategies for engineering receptor binding proteins"); Oromí-Bosch et al., 2023.
*   **(d) Specific revision required:** **[must-fix]** Delete the hyperbolic "platform for engineering" and "intelligent therapeutics" rhetoric from the Abstract, Discussion, and Conclusion. Replace it with a grounded statement indicating that the genome sequence may inform future research.

**R4-06: TRANSLATIONAL GAPS**
*   **(a) Claim/line attacked:** Lines 256–273, asserting the translational utility of Hp3 for aquaculture based entirely on an intraperitoneal (IP) injection monotherapy model.
*   **(b) Why it is vulnerable:** IP injection is virtually useless and economically non-scalable for field aquaculture; real-world applications demand immersion or oral (feed-based) delivery routes. Furthermore, the *in vitro* data (Figure 3) explicitly shows rapid bacterial regrowth (resistance) by 48 hours, yet the authors ignore the absolute necessity of integrating this phage into a cocktail for resistance management in a clinical setting. 
*   **(c) Corpus citation:** Ramos-Vivas et al., 2021 (discussing practical administration routes in aquaculture); Muliya Sankappa et al., 2024 (which successfully tested AhFM11 via immersion and top-coated feed routes).
*   **(d) Specific revision required:** **[must-fix]** Add a robust paragraph in the Discussion acknowledging severe translational limitations. Specifically, note the lack of testing via scalable delivery routes (immersion/feed) and state explicitly that the rapid bacterial resistance observed *in vitro* mandates that Hp3 must be formulated into a phage cocktail before any real-world application is viable.

**Reviewer Verdict:**
The single strongest defensible novelty claim the authors can make is the isolation, complete genomic sequencing, and *in vivo* validation of a highly divergent, unclassified genus of *Aeromonas* phage. However, the single biggest novelty-related reviewer-blocker is the manuscript's blatant omission of recent, highly relevant prior *in vivo* literature (e.g., AhFM11), which they use to artificially inflate the perceived uniqueness of their fish-rescue trial while leaning on a contested "new family" taxonomic claim to mask the fact that their biological assays are entirely routine. They must contextualize their findings honestly against the current state of the art to meet the journal's standard.
