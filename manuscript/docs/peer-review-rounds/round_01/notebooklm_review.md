## NotebookLM answer (verbatim)

Here is my peer review report on the manuscript, focusing on the rigorous evaluation of the taxonomic claims.

**R1-01**
**(a) Claim/Line attacked:** Lines 227–235 ("Hp3 branched as an isolated, deep lineage, its highest genomic similarity being only $S_G$ = 0.073 to the unclassified Rhizobium phage vB_RleS_L338C... these convergent analyses indicate that Hp3 and HJ05 constitute a new genus that represents a putative new family-level lineage.")
**(b) Why it is vulnerable:** The authors base their "new family" claim heavily on a ViPTree $S_G$ score of 0.073 and a vConTACT3 grouping with a single, highly divergent (3% nucleotide identity) unclassified phage. This is taxonomically meaningless. The ViPTree literature defines $S_G$ as a normalized tBLASTx score for generating proteomic trees, but it establishes absolutely no quantitative thresholds for family-level demarcation [1]. Furthermore, following the recent massive restructuring of phage taxonomy by the ICTV, there are no fixed quantitative nucleotide or proteome thresholds for family ranks [2]. Family demarcation currently relies on demonstrating a cohesive, monophyletic group sharing a significant number of orthologous genes [3]. Asserting a new family based on a low similarity score to one unclassified neighbor is entirely speculative and lacks the required core-gene orthology analysis.
**(c) Literature citation:** Turner et al., 2021 ("A Roadmap for Genome-Based Phage Taxonomy") [2, 3]; Nishimura et al., 2017 ("ViPTree the viral proteomic tree server") [1].
**(d) Specific revision/additional analysis required:** The authors must completely remove the assertion of a "putative new family-level lineage." The relationship to L338C should simply be described as a distant, deep-branching homology. If the authors insist on discussing family-level distinctness, they must perform a rigorous pangenome/core-gene analysis (e.g., CoreGenes) against neighboring clades to prove monophyly of shared orthologs, rather than relying on an arbitrary ViPTree score.

**R1-02**
**(a) Claim/Line attacked:** Lines 217–220 & 234–235 (Delineating Hp3 and HJ05 as a single genus, and subsequently leveraging this isolated two-genome genus to assert a "putative new family-level lineage").
**(b) Why it is vulnerable:** The proposed "family" consists of exactly two closely related genomes (Hp3 and HJ05), and HJ05 is itself an unclassified *Caudoviricetes* member. Proposing a family from two genomes whose only close relationship is to each other is highly circular and violates current taxonomic frameworks. Because the broader viral database is notoriously undersampled, isolated single genera routinely collapse into broader families once intermediary genomes are sequenced. The ICTV explicitly states that creating new families requires at least two discrete genera (ideally more) to establish robust demarcation criteria [4, 5]. 
**(c) Literature citation:** Turner et al., 2023 ("Abolishment of morphology-based taxa and change to binomial species names 2022 taxonomy update of the ICTV bacterial viruses subcommittee") [4, 5]; Turner et al., 2021 ("A Roadmap for Genome-Based Phage Taxonomy") [6].
**(d) Specific revision/additional analysis required:** The authors must explicitly concede that a new family cannot be proposed or properly delineated from a single genus containing only two genomes under current ICTV guidelines. The claim that this is a "putative new family" must be struck; it is nothing more than a divergent, undersampled genus.

**R1-03**
**(a) Claim/Line attacked:** Lines 235–238 ("This genus-level assignment was independently corroborated by amino-acid genome-BLAST distance phylogeny (VICTOR/GBDP, formula D6), in which Hp3 and HJ05 again formed an exclusive genus-level cluster...").
**(b) Why it is vulnerable:** The authors have selectively reported their data. VICTOR is explicitly designed to calculate and yield OPTSIL clustering predictions at the species, genus, subfamily, and *family* levels [7]. However, the authors suspiciously only report the genus-level output. Given that VICTOR is known to frequently exhibit a lack of support at the backbone of phylogenetic trees and for family-level resolutions [8], omitting the family-level OPTSIL prediction is highly problematic. Did VICTOR group Hp3 into a massive, polyphyletic existing family? The silence on this metric indicates methodological discordance that the authors are attempting to hide.
**(c) Literature citation:** Meier-Kolthoff & Göker, 2017 ("VICTOR - Genome-based phylogeny and classification of prokaryotic viruses") [7, 8].
**(d) Specific revision/additional analysis required:** The authors must disclose the full OPTSIL clustering results from VICTOR, explicitly stating what family-level (and subfamily-level) assignment the software predicted. Any discordance between VICTOR's family prediction, vConTACT3, and ViPTree must be transparently discussed.

**R1-04**
**(a) Claim/Line attacked:** Lines 1–2 (Title: "A novel bacteriophage representing a putative new viral family...") and Lines 234–235, 324-325.
**(b) Why it is vulnerable:** The manuscript frames this finding as a "putative new viral family" yet fails to provide an ICTV taxonomic proposal (TaxoProp), a proposed family name, or proper demarcation criteria based on shared signature genes. To a journal readership, this rhetoric is entirely non-actionable and clutters the literature with unofficial, rogue taxonomy. A single paper describing an isolate is not the venue to declare a new family without engaging the formal taxonomic process.
**(c) Literature citation:** Turner et al., 2021 ("A Roadmap for Genome-Based Phage Taxonomy") [2, 3].
**(d) Specific revision/additional analysis required:** The title must be revised to remove the phrase "representing a putative new viral family". The framing throughout the text must be downgraded to state that Hp3 forms a "deeply divergent lineage that currently remains an unclassified *Caudoviricetes* floating genus, which may warrant recognition as a new family pending future sampling and formal ICTV evaluation." 

**R1-05**
**(a) Claim/Line attacked:** Lines 275–278 ("Hp3 represents a novel viral lineage... a finding that underscores the vast, unexplored phage diversity in aquatic ecosystems").
**(b) Why it is vulnerable:** The authors overstate their novelty by failing to benchmark Hp3 against the broader context of known, unclassified aquatic and *Aeromonas* phages. The discovery of highly divergent phages that do not cluster with known families is a very common, well-documented phenomenon, particularly in *Aeromonas*. Following the abolishment of the morphological families, there are currently hundreds of unclassified, "floating" genera in the class *Caudoviricetes* [9]. Moreover, comparative genomics of *Aeromonas* phages specifically has shown that many isolates demonstrate very low nucleotide sequence coverage and identity to known databases, remaining as "outliers" or novel unclassified subgraphs [10-12].
**(c) Literature citation:** Bujak et al., 2022 ("Characterization of Three Novel Virulent Aeromonas Phages Provides Insights into the Diversity of the Autographiviridae Family") [10-12]; Turner et al., 2023 ("Abolishment of morphology-based taxa...") [9].
**(d) Specific revision/additional analysis required:** The authors must contextualize their finding within the current landscape of *Caudoviricetes* taxonomy. They must acknowledge that Hp3 simply joins an existing pool of over 600 unclassified floating genera and that extreme divergence is a recognized hallmark of *Aeromonas* phages. 

**Reviewer Verdict:**
I strongly recommend major revisions regarding the taxonomic claims of this manuscript. While the *in vivo* therapeutic efficacy data in largemouth bass appears sound, the taxonomic assertions are a textbook example of overreach. The authors have attempted to leap from a single, two-genome genus to declaring a "putative new viral family" using arbitrary metrics (ViPTree $S_G$ scores) and selective reporting (omitting VICTOR's family-level output). This directly contradicts current ICTV *Caudoviricetes* demarcation practices, which require multiple genera, robust core-gene monophyly, and formal proposals to establish new families. I will not accept this manuscript unless the authors completely remove the "new family" claim from the title and abstract, report the full results of their VICTOR analysis, and appropriately downgrade their conclusions to describe Hp3 as a deeply divergent, unclassified "orphan" or floating genus.
